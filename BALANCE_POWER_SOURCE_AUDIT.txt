MYSTIC ONSLAUGHT - PHASE 1 POWER-SOURCE AUDIT
================================================
Date: 2026-07-11
Scope: player attack, HP, mana, healing, armour, regeneration, damage multipliers,
XP pacing, and overlapping run/meta rewards. Village and editor systems excluded.

EXECUTIVE FINDINGS
------------------
The original snowball did not come from one number. A level automatically granted
HP, healing, attack, max mana, and mana refill, then immediately offered another
stat card. Shops, relics, meta unlocks, pickups, boss recovery, and class healing
stacked on top. This made every reward source independently reasonable but the
combined run much stronger than the enemy curve expected.

The largest confirmed runaway sources were: automatic all-stat level gains;
class ability levels multiplying damage by 2x and then 3x; enemy-count-based XP;
Zeph offering up to six uncapped stat purchases alongside ability purchases;
several large relic damage multipliers; permanent mana regeneration up to 2x;
and a full heal after choosing to continue beyond a boss. Phases 1-2 address the
first three. Shop, sustain, relic, and meta corrections remain later phases.

LEVEL AND POWER-CHOICE SOURCES
------------------------------
Character::AddExp previously added +1 max HP, healed 1, added +0.4 attack, added
+5 max mana, and refilled that mana on every level. GenerateLevelUpOptions then
offered three more stat cards, including attack, percentage HP, mana regeneration,
armour, speed, and mixed epic packages. This double reward was the clearest early
run snowball and also erased chip damage through automatic healing.

ApplyUpgrade contains common, rare, and epic stat packages. Before this phase,
common upgrades included +1 attack, +3 max HP plus heal, +6 mana plus refill,
+12% reach, +18 speed, and armour. Rare/epic cards can still add percentage HP,
healing, attack, mana regeneration, speed, and armour in combinations. Elite,
treasure, and shop reward contexts can still offer those stronger packages and
must be reviewed with the shop/reward economy rather than removed blindly.

Ability upgrades are another major source. Mage abilities use a small authored
upgrade bonus, but class abilities used their raw ability level as a damage
multiplier: level 2 dealt 2x base and level 3 dealt 3x base. Phase 2 changes that
class formula to +25% per ability level, producing 1.0x, 1.25x, and 1.5x.

XP SOURCES AND PACING
---------------------
Dungeon enemies previously paid individual XP based on enemy type, with elites
commonly worth 4-6 and bosses paying 10 times wave depth. More enemies therefore
meant more levels, so harder/swarm rooms also paid enough power to invalidate
later difficulty. Contracts could further multiply XP. The old Character curve
was hardcoded separately from GameBalance.h, so displayed design constants were
not the actual runtime thresholds.

Phase 2 moves the dungeon route to room-clear XP: 12 standard, 18 elite, and 30
boss XP. Legacy/wave mode retains kill XP for compatibility. GameBalance.h now
owns the level cap and threshold function. The curve begins at 20 XP and rises by
8 per level, with level 9 as the cap. Starting at level 1, this permits at most
eight Power Choices and targets roughly 6-8 over a full route.

SHOP SOURCES - PHASE 3 TARGET
-----------------------------
ShopManager currently builds a pool containing all 17 common/rare/epic stat
upgrades, shuffles it, and offers up to six stat items. It separately offers up
to six unowned class abilities. Purchases have no per-run stat-category cap, and
rerolls can search the same broad power pool. This makes gold convert too cleanly
into permanent run power and duplicates both level and relic progression.

The next shop phase should replace most inventory with tactical supplies, capped
rare stats, class-aware ability modifiers, risk bargains, and services. Direct
attack/HP/mana purchases need per-run caps and steep repeat pricing. The six
common shared effects were reduced in Phase 2 and their shop previews synchronized,
but inventory structure and purchase caps have intentionally not been rebuilt yet.

RELIC SOURCES - LATER TUNING TARGET
-----------------------------------
Relics add broad damage through Keen Edge, Bloodlust, Glass Cannon, Momentum,
Reaper, Berserker, status synergies, Executioner, and Deadeye crits. The runtime
values are generally lower than some Relic.cpp descriptions, which is a UX bug,
but several additive multipliers can coexist and Deadeye then multiplies the
result. Relic acquisition after elite/boss rewards can overlap with their stat
card reward, creating two power rewards from one encounter.

Defensive/sustain relics include extra armour capacity, immediate armour refills,
percentage max HP plus healing, flat HP plus healing, healing every five kills,
four HP on elite/boss kills, and +22 percentage points of healing-drop chance.
Arcane Battery also adds mana and regeneration. Later tuning should preserve
build identity while reducing generic damage stacking and repeated free healing.

META-PROGRESSION SOURCES - LATER TUNING TARGET
-----------------------------------------------
Poe's meta tree can grant +30/+60/+120 starting gold, retain up to 60% gold on
death, give +2/+4 starting HP, multiply mana regeneration by 1.5x/2x, add one
starting armour, unlock fifth and sixth ability slots, grant a random starting
relic, and provide a once-per-run revive at 40% HP. These are direct permanent
advantages, so a completed profile begins each run substantially ahead of a new
profile instead of mainly having broader build choices.

The largest meta risks are 2x mana regeneration, +4 HP relative to single-digit
class health, a free relic, and Second Wind. A future meta pass should shift most
of this toward unlock breadth, village utility, alternate starting choices, and
information rather than raw combat superiority. No meta values changed in
Phases 1-2 so existing saves remain compatible while the run baseline is tested.

HEALING, MANA, AND ARMOUR SOURCES - PHASE 4 TARGET
--------------------------------------------------
Healing comes from one-HP enemy drops, timed drops, Scavenger's drop bonus,
Vampirism, Reaper, Bulwark, HP upgrade cards, War Cry, low-health recovery when
entering Zeph's room, Second Wind, and a full heal when continuing after a boss.
The full boss heal and repeated kill/drop healing are the strongest sustain
loops. Phase 2 removes automatic level healing and common Max Health card healing.

Mana regenerates passively from a class base, then scales through Arcane Mind,
Arcane Colossus, Arcane Battery, and permanent Mana Flow meta unlocks. Max-mana
upgrades also previously refilled mana. Phase 2 removes the common Max Mana card's
refill, but regeneration multipliers remain. Armour is both a refill resource and
an expandable capacity through relics/meta, so it can absorb many mistakes when
combined with healing and should be measured in telemetry before further cuts.

PHASE 2 IMPLEMENTED BASELINE
----------------------------
- GameBalance.h is the runtime source for max level and XP thresholds.
- Dungeon XP is awarded for clearing rooms, not for every enemy spawned.
- Levels no longer automatically add HP, attack, mana, healing, or mana refill.
- Normal levels are called Power Choices and prioritize learned ability upgrades.
- Remaining Power Choice cards are modest defense/utility options, with no flat
  attack card forced into the normal pool.
- Class ability upgrades now scale at +25% per level instead of 2x/3x damage.
- Common attack/range/HP/mana/speed gains were reduced, and HP/mana no longer
  heal or refill when those common upgrades are selected.
- Card and Zeph preview values were synchronized with the changed common effects.

VALIDATION QUESTIONS FOR PLAYTESTING
------------------------------------
Record player level after each act, number of Power Choices offered, chosen cards,
room clear time, damage taken, healing gained, mana starvation/overflow, and boss
fight duration. A healthy first target is 1-2 choices in Act 1, 6-8 across a full
run, weak enemies dying in 2-4 basics, and mistakes remaining visible across more
than one room. If progression feels too slow, adjust room-clear XP in GameBalance.h
before restoring kill XP or automatic stats.

Do not tune enemy HP upward until this baseline has been played. If the player is
still too strong, inspect shop purchases, relic combinations, full boss healing,
meta mana/HP, and heal-drop counts in that order. Those systems currently have a
larger combined effect than the modest enemy health curve.

IMPROVEMENT PASS ADDENDUM (second audit sweep, 2026-07-11 evening)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Additional findings from a deeper file-level sweep, plus what the improvement
pass already fixed in code. Line references are approximate after edits.

NEWLY DOCUMENTED POWER SOURCES (missed above; mostly Phase 4 targets):
1. FULL HEAL ON EVERY ROOM ENTRY - the single biggest sustain lever in the game.
   Character::Revive() sets health to max and is called from the dungeon
   room-entry path (Engine.cpp ~768). Damage therefore only matters within one
   room; inter-room attrition is zero. Phase 4 must split Revive() into a true
   respawn (full heal, keep) and a RefreshForRoomEntry() (state reset, NO heal).
   Other Revive call sites: debug tile-room entry (~882), village entry (~2522,
   correct to keep), dungeon-view debug entry (~16651).
2. LIFESTEAL COMPOUNDING - GrantLifesteal callers: Warrior Rampage 0.25/7s,
   Rogue Blade Dance 0.3/6s, Warlock Drain Life 0.6 burst + 0.25/5s lingering,
   Warlock Demon Form 0.35/8s (Engine.cpp ApplyPlayerLifesteal drains it). A %
   of damage dealt scales with every damage buff, so damage growth buys sustain
   automatically. Phase 4 candidate: per-room lifesteal cap rather than nerfing
   the class identity grants themselves.
3. DEAD META CONTENT - the Deep Pockets gold-retention unlocks (20/40/60%) are
   fully overridden by the one-wallet death wipe (Engine.cpp ~8984 comment).
   Players can spend Echoes on an unlock that does nothing. Either hide the
   three cards or repurpose them (e.g. small starting-gold bonus) in the meta
   pass. Do not reorder the unlock table - saves store it.
4. BOSS XP in legacy/wave mode still pays 10 x wave (CombatDirector.cpp ~840),
   uncapped - fine for the demo arena, but do not port it back to dungeon mode.

FIXED IN THE IMPROVEMENT PASS (code changes, Debug|x64 clean):
- GameBalance.h truth pass: deleted the dead Balance::Player block (real stats
  live in PlayerClass.cpp kClassTable; the old constants were never read) and
  promoted the hardcoded class-ability level step (0.25f in Engine's dmgVal)
  to Balance::AbilityDamage::kClassLevelStep.
- Rare/epic Power Choice PREVIEW DRIFT: card texts promised far more than
  ApplyUpgrade delivers (Iron Constitution +25% shown vs x1.15 real; Swift Feet
  x1.15 vs x1.08; Arcane Mind +25 mana vs +8; War God x1.20 vs +15%; Resilience
  +30% vs x1.18; Blade Storm x1.18/x1.18 vs +1.5/x1.08; Juggernaut +20%/+1 vs
  x1.12/+2; Iron Skin claimed 1 armour but grants 2). All previews now state
  the real effect. Commons were already synchronized in the first Phase 2 pass.
- RELIC DESCRIPTION DRIFT fixed in Relic.cpp (code is canon, per design call):
  Ember Heart/Permafrost/Overcharge 40-60% -> real +25%; Executioner +120% ->
  +35%; Deadeye double -> +50% crit; Glass Cannon +80% -> +35%; Second Wind
  +30% -> +20%; Swift Boots +14% -> +8%; Arcane Battery +40/+40% -> +8/+12%.
- Character.h UpgradeType enum comments corrected to the real values.

NEW POWER CHOICE CARDS (Phase 2 "build identity" improvement):
- Mana Flow (common): +1 max mana, +15% mana regen. Tempo compensation for the
  removed +5-mana-per-level without re-inflating the pool.
- Class Attunement (rare): +25% class resource gain (Warrior Rage / Paladin
  Faith / Rogue Combo Points, with a fractional bank so +25% is real for combo
  points). Only offered to those three classes.
- Overload (rare, TRADEOFF): +15% class ability damage but every cast costs
  +1 mana. Routed through Character::GetAbilityCost so the charge site, the
  cast gate, the HUD ready-check, and Zeph's inspect card all show/charge the
  real surcharged cost. Ultimates (drain-all) are exempt.
- Pool shape per level-up: up to 2 learned-ability upgrade cards, then a 45%
  chance one slot is a build-shaping rare (Attunement/Overload), remaining
  slots from the modest utility pool (now including Mana Flow).
TUNING POINTS: the 45% build-card chance, Overload stack behavior (each stack
+15% dmg / +1 mana - self-limiting since base costs are 1-4), and Attunement
stacking (uncapped for now; watch Paladin Faith uptime in telemetry).
