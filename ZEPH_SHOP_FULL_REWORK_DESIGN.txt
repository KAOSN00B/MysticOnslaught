MYSTIC ONSLAUGHT - ZEPH'S SHOP FULL INVENTORY REWORK
====================================================
Date: 2026-07-11
Status: Design and implementation specification
Scope: What Zeph sells, how stock is generated, prices, limits, effects, and code
structure. The current shop screen layout and general visual presentation stay.

1. PURPOSE
----------
Zeph's shop should remain one of the most important breaks in a dungeon run. It
gives the player a moment to breathe, inspect the build, spend earned gold, and
make a decision about the next stretch. The problem is not the current UI. The
problem is that the inventory converts gold into too much clean permanent power.
Buying attack, max HP, max mana, speed, regeneration, armour, epic stat packages,
and new abilities from the same shop makes Zeph a second level-up system.

The new shop should feel closer to a clever roguelite merchant. Most purchases
should solve a short-term problem, alter an ability, reshape the build, or ask
the player to accept a risk. Permanent raw stats can remain because they are
easy to understand and occasionally exciting, but they must be rare, expensive,
and capped. A player should leave thinking, "I changed my plan," rather than,
"all my numbers went up again."

This rework deliberately preserves the existing two-tab, six-card layout:

- WARES: tactical supplies, services, risk bargains, and scarce rare stats.
- ABILITIES: new abilities, upgrades/modifiers for owned abilities, and one
  class-signature offer when possible.

Only small card-label additions are required. The screen dimensions, Zeph art,
navigation, buy buttons, before/after area, reroll button, and general placement
can remain as they are.

2. DESIGN PILLARS
-----------------
PILLAR A - EVERY PURCHASE HAS A JOB

An item should answer a recognizable need: survive the next room, improve a
specific move, repair a weak build, trade safety for a payoff, change an ability,
or prepare for a boss. Generic bundles that simply improve everything should be
removed from Zeph's normal stock.

PILLAR B - TEMPORARY POWER IS ALLOWED TO FEEL STRONG

A next-room item can be noticeably powerful because it expires. This lets Zeph
sell exciting effects without permanently trivializing the rest of the run.
Temporary purchases also make the player look at the map and plan when to spend.

PILLAR C - PERMANENT POWER IS SCARCE

Permanent attack, health, mana, armour capacity, and regeneration should never
fill six slots. A run may buy one Rare Stat item from Zeph, with perhaps a second
allowed only through a costly bargain. This keeps the shop useful without letting
gold become an unlimited stat conversion resource.

PILLAR D - CLASS IDENTITY COMES FIRST

Ability stock must understand the active class and the abilities the player owns.
A Warrior should see Rage decisions. A Rogue should see combo and poison choices.
A Hunter should see mark, projectile, and trap choices. Paladin offers should use
Faith, armour, and reflection. Warlock offers should use curse, health cost, and
drain. Mage offers should emphasize elemental sequencing rather than three copies
of the same damage spell in different colours.

PILLAR E - THE SHOP NEVER SOLVES THE RUN

Zeph may stabilize a bad run, but should not fully heal the player, fully restore
all resources, max every ability, and add permanent damage during one visit.
The player must choose what matters most. Gold should run out before all desirable
options are purchased.

3. CURRENT SYSTEM DIAGNOSIS
---------------------------
The current `GenerateInventory` function creates a pool containing all seventeen
common, rare, and epic stat upgrades. It shuffles that pool and places up to six
on WARES. It also finds unowned non-ultimate abilities for the active class and
places up to six on ABILITIES. A complete visit can therefore expose twelve
permanent run upgrades before rerolls.

The current pool includes direct attack, range, max HP, max mana, armour, speed,
percentage attack, percentage HP, mana regeneration, combined damage/range,
combined damage/speed, combined HP/armour, and combined mana/damage/regeneration.
There are no per-run category caps. Rerolls search the same power pool again.
Act inflation raises prices, but it does not fix the underlying issue because a
successful player also earns more gold and can still buy several clean upgrades.

Abilities are also treated only as owned or unowned. Zeph cannot improve the
shape of an owned move, create a tradeoff, swap a move, or help a build that
already has full slots. This makes the ABILITIES tab less useful later in a run
while the WARES tab remains an unrestricted stat source.

4. TARGET VISIT STRUCTURE
-------------------------
Each shop visit should generate exactly six WARES and up to six ABILITIES. Stock
must be assembled by slot rules, not by shuffling one giant list.

WARES SLOT RECIPE

Slot 1: Tactical Supply - defensive or emergency preparation.
Slot 2: Tactical Supply - offensive, mana, scouting, or utility preparation.
Slot 3: Service - reshape, reserve, cleanse, salvage, or reveal.
Slot 4: Risk Bargain - strong upside with a visible cost.
Slot 5: Flex - another supply, service, or bargain based on player state.
Slot 6: Rare Shelf - usually a premium item; may contain a capped Rare Stat,
        premium service, relic offer, or high-risk class item.

ABILITIES SLOT RECIPE

Slot 1: Modifier for an owned normal ability.
Slot 2: Modifier for a different owned normal ability, when possible.
Slot 3: Class-signature modifier using Rage, Combo, Mark, Faith, Curse, or Elements.
Slot 4: One unowned class ability, if the player has a legal slot or swap path.
Slot 5: Upgrade/modifier, replacement service, or second new ability.
Slot 6: Rare ability offer, advanced modifier, or locked until later acts.

When a required pool is empty, the generator substitutes from a defined fallback
pool. It must never duplicate the same item ID in one visit. It should also avoid
offering two mutually exclusive modifiers for the same ability unless the point
of the offer is explicitly to choose between them.

5. ITEM CATEGORIES
------------------
Every shop item needs an explicit category. Category determines its stock slot,
price band, persistence, purchase cap, and card badge.

TACTICAL SUPPLY

A run effect lasting for the next combat room, next boss, or a small number of
uses. Supplies may be strong because they expire. They should not silently turn
into permanent stats. Unused next-room supplies persist until the player enters
a combat room, so buying one before a non-combat room does not waste it.

BUILD MODIFIER

A permanent-for-this-run change to one ability or class mechanic. Most modifiers
should include a tradeoff, limitation, or narrower purpose. A modifier should
change how the player uses the move, not merely say "+20% damage." Modifiers are
the main long-term power Zeph sells.

NEW ABILITY

An unowned, non-ultimate ability valid for the active class and meta unlocks.
New abilities remain useful, but the shop offers at most two per visit and no
more than two purchased from Zeph per run. Boss and Power Choice rewards remain
important sources of ability growth.

RARE STAT

A small permanent stat increase. Only one Rare Stat purchase is allowed per run.
Rare Stats are expensive, cannot receive the Daily Deal discount, and do not
reappear after the cap is reached. They should never be percentage packages that
grow stronger because the player already stacked the stat.

RISK BARGAIN

A strong immediate or permanent upside paired with a specific downside. The
downside must appear on the card before purchase and must not be hidden in small
text. Some bargains cost little or no gold because risk is their real price.

SERVICE

A one-time operation on the current build or run: swap an ability, reserve an
item, cleanse a drawback, reroll one card, reveal map information, salvage a
relic, or bank currency. Services create value without adding raw combat power.

6. WARES - FIRST IMPLEMENTATION CATALOGUE
-----------------------------------------
The following catalogue is intentionally larger than six items so visits vary.
The first implementation should begin with the simpler entries marked CORE.

Effect: Elite and dangerous support enemies are outlined for the next room.

Effect: Restore 2 mana now. Does not increase max mana or regeneration.
Condition: Cannot be bought at full mana; one per visit.
Base price: 30 gold.

SERVICES

CORE - Reserve Stock
Effect: Lock one unpurchased card so it survives the next reroll.
Base price: 15 gold.
Limit: One reserved card at a time.
Purpose: Reduces frustrating randomness without granting power by itself.

CORE - Forget and Learn
Effect: Replace one owned non-ultimate ability with one offered unowned ability.
The new ability enters at level 1. The removed ability's upgrades are lost.
Base price: 65 gold plus the new ability's normal price.
Purpose: Lets a full build pivot rather than making the tab useless.

CORE - Clean Contract
Effect: Remove one optional contract/bargain downside. Does not remove Ascension
rules, boss mechanics, or biome rules.
Base price: 80 gold.
Purpose: A costly escape valve for a run-specific risk.

Targeted Reroll
Effect: Reroll one selected unpurchased card instead of the full shop.
Base price: 30 gold, then +25 for each use in the same visit.
Purpose: Gives control without repeatedly fishing six slots at once.

Relic Appraisal
Effect: Reveal three relics and choose one to replace an owned relic. The old
relic is destroyed. No net increase to relic count.
Base price: 120 gold.
Limit: Once per run.
Purpose: Repairs a mismatched build without adding another relic stack.

Cell Banking
Effect: Bank carried cells immediately, but Zeph keeps 25% of the amount.
Base price: No gold; the conversion loss is the price.
Purpose: A safety decision between meta progress and run greed.

RISK BARGAINS

CORE - Blood Price
Effect: Upgrade one owned normal ability by one level.
Downside: Lose 1 max HP for the rest of the run; current HP clamps if necessary.
Gold price: 40.
Limit: Once per run.
Purpose: Strong build direction with a permanent, understandable cost.

CORE - Hunted Purse
Effect: Gain 60 gold now.
Downside: The next combat room adds an Assassin or Ranged pressure enemy and
enemies attack 10% faster.
Gold price: 0.
Purpose: Lets a poor player engage with the shop by borrowing against difficulty.

CORE - Zeph's Wager
Effect: Next combat room awards +50% gold and +25% room-clear XP.
Downside: Enemies move 12% faster and the room spawns one extra role threat.
Gold price: 20.
Purpose: Voluntary difficulty for players who want to accelerate the run.

Overchannel Note
Effect: Max mana +4 for the rest of the run.
Downside: Passive mana regeneration is 15% slower.
Gold price: 80.
Purpose: Larger planned bursts versus slower recovery.

Glass Contract
Effect: Deal 18% more damage during the next boss fight.
Downside: Healing pickups cannot spawn during that fight.
Gold price: 55.
Purpose: Faster boss attempt at the cost of forgiveness.

Collector's Debt
Effect: Take one Rare Shelf item for 50% price.
Downside: The next 60 gold collected is paid to Zeph before entering the wallet.
Limit: One active debt.
Purpose: Timing flexibility without creating free value.

RARE STATS

CORE - Tempered Heart
Effect: +1 max HP. Does not heal.
Base price: 150 gold.

CORE - Honed Edge
Effect: +0.5 attack power.
Base price: 180 gold.

CORE - Deep Reservoir
Effect: +4 max mana. Does not refill mana.
Base price: 145 gold.

Reinforced Harness
Effect: +1 maximum armour capacity and fill that new slot.
Base price: 210 gold.

Rare Stat rules:
- Only one Rare Stat may be purchased from Zeph per run.
- Rare Stats do not receive Daily Deal discounts.
- Rare Stats cannot appear in Act 1's first shop unless generated in Slot 6.
- Percentage HP, percentage attack, and combined epic stat packages are removed
  from normal shop stock.
- Once the cap is reached, Slot 6 becomes a premium modifier, relic service, or
  high-tier tactical supply.

7. ABILITIES - STOCK RULES
--------------------------
The ABILITIES tab should become useful at every stage of a run. Early shops can
help the player learn a second or third move. Later shops should mostly modify
owned moves and class mechanics. A full ability bar should trigger swap services,
not an empty or unusable tab.

NEW ABILITY RULES

- Only abilities legal for the active class and unlocked through meta progression.
- Never sell ultimates; boss rewards remain the main ultimate source.
- Offer at most two unowned abilities per visit.
- A player may buy at most two new abilities from Zeph per run.
- Base price: 100 for normal abilities, 135 for high-impact bolts/zones.
- Second Zeph ability purchased in a run costs +50%.
- If normal slots are full, buying opens the existing replace-ability flow.
- New abilities always enter at level 1.

ABILITY UPGRADE RULES

- A plain level upgrade may appear, but at most one per visit.
- First purchased ability level: 100 gold.
- Second purchased ability level in the same run: 170 gold.
- Third purchased ability level in the same run: 260 gold.
- No ability may exceed level 3.
- Plain level upgrades compete with modifiers; they do not get a separate free
  inventory lane.

BUILD MODIFIER RULES

- Maximum three Zeph modifiers per run.
- Maximum two modifiers attached to the same ability.
- Mutually exclusive modifiers use a shared group ID and cannot coexist.
- A card always names the affected ability and states both upside and downside.
- Base price: 90 common, 135 rare, 190 signature.
- Modifiers are permanent for the run unless explicitly marked next-room/boss.
- Rerolls should weight offers toward owned abilities that have no modifier yet.

9. INVENTORY GENERATION ALGORITHM
---------------------------------
Inventory generation should be deterministic for the visit seed and assembled
in steps. This prevents a reroll, pause, or save/load from changing stock without
the player paying for it.

Step 1: Build a `ShopGenerationContext` containing class, act, room depth, current
HP/mana, gold, known abilities and levels, modifiers already owned, run purchase
caps, relics, active contracts, reroll count, and whether a boss is nearby.

Step 2: Filter every item definition by eligibility. Examples: Emergency Stitch
requires low HP; a trap modifier requires the trap; Clean Contract requires an
optional downside; Rare Stats require the run cap to be unused; a new ability
requires class/meta eligibility; max-level abilities reject plain upgrades.

Step 3: Fill mandatory category slots using weighted pools. The generator should
weight useful state responses without guaranteeing them. Low HP increases the
weight of defensive supplies, low mana increases Freecast/Mana Shard, a full
ability bar increases swap services, and an upcoming boss increases boss supplies.

Step 4: Enforce uniqueness, mutual exclusion, and target diversity. Two ability
modifier slots should prefer different abilities. A modifier already owned should
not return unless explicitly stackable.

Step 5: Set prices from base price, act inflation, repeat-purchase surcharge, and
item-specific rules. Price is stored on the generated item so previews and saves
remain stable.

Step 6: Choose one Daily Deal only from Tactical Supplies, Services, non-signature
Build Modifiers, or new abilities. Never discount Rare Stats, debt repayments,
free bargains, or emergency healing.

Step 7: Save stock IDs, targets, prices, purchased flags, and the visit seed.

10. PRICING AND GOLD ECONOMY
----------------------------
Recommended base bands:

- Information and small utility: 15-30 gold.
- Tactical next-room power: 30-60 gold.
- Emergency healing: 55-70 gold.
- Services: 30-120 gold depending on permanence.
- New normal ability: 100-135 gold.
- Common ability modifier: 90-115 gold.
- Rare/signature modifier: 135-190 gold.
- Plain ability level upgrade: 100, then 170, then 260 per run.
- Rare Stat: 145-210 gold and one per run.
- Risk Bargain: 0-80 gold because the downside carries part of the price.

Act inflation should be reduced from 25% to approximately 15% per act for
temporary supplies and services. Permanent effects may use 20% per act. The
current 25% blanket inflation makes late utility unattractive while successful
players can still afford premium permanent power. Category-specific inflation
keeps basic tools relevant throughout the run.

Reroll rules:

- Full-stock reroll starts at 50 gold and increases by 50 each use.
- Maximum two full rerolls per shop visit.
- Purchased cards stay purchased; reroll replaces only unsold cards.
- A reserved card survives the reroll.
- Rerolls do not reset emergency-item limits or run purchase caps.
- The same item cannot return immediately after being rerolled away.
- Targeted Reroll is separate and escalates 30, 55, 80.

11. POOR-PLAYER AND BAD-RNG PROTECTION
--------------------------------------
Every visit should offer a decision even at zero gold, but not free healing or
free permanent power. The guaranteed Risk Bargain slot serves this purpose.
Hunted Purse gives spending money in exchange for a harder next room. Zeph's
Wager lets a confident player buy into better future rewards. Cell Banking gives
safety at a conversion loss. These options create engagement without rewarding
arriving poor more than arriving prepared.

The shop also needs anti-frustration rules. At least one WARES item should cost
60 gold or less. At least one ABILITIES card should be usable with the current
build. If no new abilities or legal modifiers exist, the tab should generate
plain upgrades, replacement services, or a class-signature modifier rather than
showing empty cards.

Do not grant a free reroll each visit. A free reroll encourages fishing and makes
the first stock feel disposable. Instead, generate purposeful stock and provide
Reserve Stock plus Targeted Reroll as controlled tools.

13. EFFECT HOOKS
----------------
Shop effects should be applied through a small number of explicit hooks rather
than scattered name checks.

- OnShopPurchase: immediate healing, mana, ability replacement, banking.
- OnCombatRoomStart: activate next-room armour, free cast, cooldown, damage.
- OnAbilityCast: first-cast-free and ability modifier behavior.
- OnAbilityHit: pierce, status, mark, curse, Rage/Faith/combo effects.
- OnEnemyKilled: spreading curse, Soul Credit, bargain rewards.
- OnCombatRoomClear: expire supplies, resolve wagers, collect debts.
- OnBossStart/End: next-boss items and healing restrictions.
- OnGoldCollected: Collector's Debt repayment before wallet gain.

The modifier query should look like `HasAbilityModifier(AbilityType, ModifierId)`
or return a compact modifier set for the ability. Avoid one boolean field per
shop item in Character. A data-driven list keeps future modifiers manageable.

14. UI COMPATIBILITY
--------------------
The current UI layout stays. Only the information presented on existing cards
needs to become more explicit.

WARES remains the left tab and ABILITIES remains the right tab. WARES cards can
show a small category label such as SUPPLY, SERVICE, BARGAIN, or RARE STAT in the
existing rarity-label area. ABILITIES cards can show NEW, MODIFIER, UPGRADE, or
SIGNATURE. This does not require moving panels or changing card dimensions.

Every card needs:

- item name
- category badge
- affected ability when relevant
- duration: NEXT ROOM, NEXT BOSS, or THIS RUN
- concise upside
- downside in a contrasting colour when present
- price
- purchase cap state such as `1/RUN`
- before/after values only when a numeric preview is genuinely useful

The card should never display a false stat preview. Modifiers should describe
behavior, not force themselves into `4 -> 4` formatting. Disabled cards should
state the reason: cap reached, ability not owned, debt active, full health, or
already modified.

15. WHAT LEAVES THE NORMAL SHOP
-------------------------------
The following current items should no longer be generated as ordinary WARES:

- Iron Constitution
- Swift Feet
- Ferocity
- Arcane Mind
- Blade Edge
- War God's Mark
- Last Stand Plate / Resilience
- Blade Storm Rig
- Juggernaut Frame
- Overchannel Core / Arcane Colossus

These packages combine too many permanent benefits and duplicate Power Choices
and relics. Their names/icons can be reused for future modifiers or Rare Shelf
items, but their existing effects should not remain in the normal random pool.

The simple Attack Power, Max Health, Max Mana, and armour-related effects move to
the one-per-run Rare Stat shelf at smaller fixed values. Attack Range and Move
Speed are better expressed as ability-specific modifiers or temporary supplies.
Emergency Plate should become a temporary armour refill, not a permanent armour
capacity purchase.

Do not delete `UpgradeType` or these effects globally. Level, treasure, debug,
and legacy reward systems may still reference them. The shop rework should stop
sourcing them from its inventory and migrate other systems separately.

16. IMPLEMENTATION PHASES
-------------------------
SHOP PHASE A - FOUNDATION

Add category, duration, item definition, generated offer, and run purchase-state
types. Keep the current draw code working through a compatibility adapter. Move
prices and inventory rules into `GameBalance.h` or a dedicated `ShopCatalog` file.
Do not implement all modifiers yet.

SHOP PHASE B - CORE WARES

Implement the six-slot recipe with CORE supplies, services, bargains, and four
Rare Stats. Add one-per-run Rare Stat cap, next-room effect lifecycle, Hunted
Purse, Zeph's Wager, Reserve Stock, and truthful category badges. Remove combined
stat packages from generated stock.

SHOP PHASE C - ABILITY TAB

Add owned-ability modifiers, new-ability purchase cap, escalating plain-upgrade
prices, and replacement flow for full slots. Begin with two CORE modifiers per
class. Keep ultimates out of shop stock.

SHOP PHASE D - ECONOMY AND REROLLS

Implement category-specific inflation, full-reroll limits, reservation, targeted
rerolls, Daily Deal eligibility, and anti-repeat rules. Confirm that purchased
offers and run caps persist across rerolls and room transitions.

SHOP PHASE E - ADVANCED SERVICES AND BARGAINS

Add relic replacement, debt, contract cleansing, cell banking, boss contracts,
and the remaining class modifiers only after the core inventory is stable.

SHOP PHASE F - TELEMETRY AND TUNING

Record shop visits, generated categories, purchases, skipped items, rerolls,
gold entering/leaving, Rare Stat count, modifiers chosen, and performance in the
next room. Tune price and offer weights from real runs rather than intuition.

17. TEST PLAN
-------------
FUNCTIONAL TESTS

- WARES generates six legal offers using the slot recipe.
- ABILITIES never offers an off-class, locked, owned-as-new, or ultimate ability.
- No duplicate item IDs appear in a visit.
- Ability modifiers only target owned legal abilities.
- Mutually exclusive modifiers cannot coexist.
- Rare Stat purchase cap is one per run and survives future visits/rerolls.
- New ability and plain-upgrade escalating prices persist for the run.
- Full ability slots open replacement instead of losing the purchase.
- Next-room supplies activate only when combat starts and expire on clear.
- Next-boss supplies survive normal rooms and expire after the boss.
- Bargain downsides activate at the same moment as their upside.
- Debt intercepts collected gold correctly and cannot make gold negative.
- Rerolls preserve purchased/reserved cards and respect the two-reroll cap.
- Daily Deal never selects forbidden categories.
- Save/load or leaving/re-entering does not regenerate unpaid stock.

BALANCE TESTS

- A normal visit should create at least three desirable choices but the player
  should usually afford only one or two.
- A zero-gold player still has a meaningful bargain/service decision.
- A rich player cannot buy more than one permanent Rare Stat per run.
- No shop visit fully restores both health and mana.
- A modifier changes play behavior and is noticeable in the next room.
- An ability-focused run remains viable without buying raw attack.
- Rerolls do not reliably produce a perfect build.
- Shop spending delays or prevents other purchases instead of being automatic.
- Players continue to value boss rewards, Power Choices, and relics after Zeph.

REGRESSION TESTS

- Existing shop mouse, keyboard, gamepad, and touch navigation still work.
- Existing card text remains inside card/button bounds.
- Village Zeph and dungeon Zeph open the same valid shop behavior where intended.
- Debug shop entry and UI editor continue to work.
- Existing ability replacement and purchase messages remain accurate.
- `Debug|x64` builds after each implementation phase.

18. TELEMETRY TARGETS
---------------------
For each visit, log:

- act, room, class, player level, HP, mana, gold
- generated offer IDs/categories/prices
- purchased offer IDs and final cost
- rerolls and targeted rerolls used
- gold left after visit
- Rare Stat, modifier, and Zeph-ability counts for the run
- next room clear time and damage taken
- whether the purchased tactical effect triggered and how much value it produced

Useful first targets:

- Average purchases per visit: 1.2 to 2.0.
- Visits with no purchase: below 20%, excluding intentional saving.
- Rare Stat purchases: no more than one per run by rule.
- Full rerolls: usually zero or one; two should be an expensive commitment.
- No single item purchased in more than 45% of eligible appearances.
- At least 60% of purchased modifiers should measurably change ability usage.

19. RECOMMENDED FIRST PLAYABLE VERSION
-------------------------------------
Do not attempt the entire catalogue in one code change. The first playable shop
should contain:

WARES
- Iron Token
- Freecast Seal
- Field Whetstone
- Focus Draught
- Surveyor's Chalk
- Emergency Stitch
- Reserve Stock
- Forget and Learn
- Clean Contract
- Blood Price
- Hunted Purse
- Zeph's Wager
- the four capped Rare Stats

ABILITIES
- legal new non-ultimate abilities
- plain owned-ability upgrades with escalating prices
- two CORE modifiers for each class
- replacement flow when slots are full

This first version is enough to prove the new fantasy. It gives poor players a
risk option, gives rich players constrained premium choices, makes late ability
tabs useful, and removes the six-card wall of permanent stat packages. Advanced
debt, relic, map, and contract systems can follow after playtesting.

20. FINAL DESIGN RULE
---------------------
Before adding any Zeph item, ask:

"Does this create a decision or merely make every number larger?"

If it only makes the player generically stronger, it belongs as a rare capped
reward, a temporary supply, or somewhere outside the shop. Zeph's purpose is to
reshape the run, prepare the player for danger, and tempt them into interesting
risks. He should not sell the solution to the dungeon.
