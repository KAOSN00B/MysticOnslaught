Mystic Onslaught - Room Interaction Ideas
=========================================

Purpose
-------
The dungeon should not become a chain of identical rooms where the only verb is:
enter room -> enemies spawn -> kill enemies -> doors open.

However, not every room should have a big special loop either. Standard combat rooms are important.
They give pacing, let the player feel their build, and keep the dungeon from becoming exhausting.

The design target is:
- Some rooms are standard combat rooms.
- Some rooms have combat events/objectives.
- Some rooms are non-combat decision rooms.
- Every room should still offer at least one point of engagement.

A point of engagement can be:
- an enemy mix that changes tactics
- an interesting layout
- a shrine or altar
- a dangerous reward choice
- a route/planning decision
- a prop or hazard to use
- a secret or suspicious object
- a build-altering opportunity
- a future-risk decision

The game should feel closer to a roguelite challenge like Dead Cells, not a dungeon crawler that hands out safe recovery rooms too often.
Non-combat rooms should not be free comfort. They should convert pressure into choice.

Core rule:
Non-combat rooms should not reduce pressure for free.
They should ask the player to trade, gamble, commit, corrupt, reroute, or accept future danger.


Chosen Non-Combat Room Direction
--------------------------------
The strongest non-combat room ideas for Mystic Onslaught are:

1. Cursed Reward Room
2. Mutation / Build Choice Room
3. Risk Shrine Room
4. Relic Forge Room
5. Locked Armory Room
6. Omen / Prophecy Room

These fit because they are:
- mid-dungeon appropriate
- roguelite-flavored
- tense without needing combat
- useful for build identity
- risky enough to avoid making the game too easy
- reusable across biomes
- compatible with future challenge-room systems

These should replace or heavily de-emphasize soft healing/rest rooms.
Healing can exist, but it should almost always have a cost, drawback, or opportunity cost.


1. Cursed Reward Room
---------------------
Fantasy:
A quiet room with a reward sitting in the open, but the room is clearly cursed.
The player can leave safely, or take the reward and accept a punishment.

Core interaction:
- Show 1-3 tempting rewards.
- Each reward has a curse attached.
- Player can take one reward or leave.
- The curse lasts for a set number of rooms, until the next boss, or for the whole biome depending on strength.

Possible rewards:
- gold pile
- relic
- ability upgrade card
- max mana increase
- max health increase
- Echoes/cells
- rare currency
- high-tier chest
- random class ability upgrade

Possible curses:
- Next 2 rooms: enemies deal +20% damage.
- Next 2 rooms: enemies move +25% faster.
- Next room: one extra elite modifier appears.
- Next room: doors stay locked until all hazards/objectives are cleared.
- Next 3 rooms: healing pickups do not spawn.
- Next 2 rooms: mana regen reduced by 50%.
- Until next boss: player takes +1 damage from traps/hazards.
- Next room: enemy projectiles are faster.
- Next room: room affix chance becomes 100%.
- Boss gains +10% HP, but drops bonus reward.

Visual language:
- A central cursed chest, statue, altar, or sealed relic.
- Purple/red/black aura.
- Floating warning text: TAKE POWER / ACCEPT CURSE.
- Reward cards should show both the reward and the curse.

Example choices:
- Take 150 gold. Curse: next room enemies gain haste.
- Take a relic. Curse: lose 10 max HP for this biome.
- Take an ability upgrade. Curse: next 2 rooms have room hazards.
- Take 3 Echoes. Curse: boss gains armor.

Why it fits:
This is a pure roguelite decision. It is not free value. It tempts the player and lets confident players increase risk for power.

Implementation notes:
- Needs a room type or room event: CursedReward.
- Needs reward cards or simple UI prompts.
- Needs a curse state that can persist across rooms.
- Curses should be visible in HUD while active.
- Room should be persistent: leaving/re-entering should not reroll rewards.

Debug needs:
- Force Cursed Reward room.
- Force each curse type.
- Clear active curses.


2. Mutation / Build Choice Room
-------------------------------
Fantasy:
A strange altar, parasite, mirror, or arcane machine lets the player reshape their build.
This is not a normal upgrade room. It changes the character in a meaningful way, usually with a drawback.

Core interaction:
- Player sees 2-3 mutation offers.
- Each offer changes a build rule.
- Each offer has a cost, downside, or lockout.
- Player can pick one or leave.

Possible mutation types:
- Sacrifice max HP for cooldown reduction.
- Sacrifice max mana for basic attack damage.
- Convert armor/defense into crit chance.
- Reduce move speed slightly for increased damage.
- Lock one ability slot for 2 rooms to empower another ability.
- Reroll one ability upgrade into another.
- Change one learned ability into a different ability from the same class/path.
- Increase lifesteal, but reduce healing from pickups.
- Gain bonus damage while below 40% HP, but max HP is reduced.
- Gain faster mana regen, but abilities cost more mana.

Examples:
- Glass Focus: -10 max HP, +15% ability damage.
- Burning Mind: +25% mana regen, +20% mana costs.
- Heavy Soul: +20% damage, -10% move speed.
- Blood Tempo: cooldowns recover faster at low HP, but healing is reduced.
- Overchannel: empower slot 1 for 3 rooms, disable slot 4 for 3 rooms.

Why it fits:
This creates build identity. It gives the player a reason to think about their current class, abilities, relics, and risk tolerance.

Visual language:
- Mirror room, mutation altar, arcane machine, or living shrine.
- Cards should clearly show upside and downside.
- Player should see current stats affected by the choice if possible.

Implementation notes:
- Some mutations can be temporary, which is safer for balance.
- Permanent run mutations should be rare and clearly costly.
- Start with simple stat-based mutations before ability-slot locking or rerolls.
- Needs a mutation list and active mutation state.
- Needs UI similar to level-up cards but with downside text.

Good first mutation set:
- +15% damage, -10 max HP.
- +20% mana regen, -15% max mana.
- +15% move speed, enemies in next 2 rooms move +10% faster.
- -15% cooldowns, +15% mana cost.

Debug needs:
- Force Mutation room.
- Apply/remove each mutation.
- Show active mutations in debug/HUD.


3. Risk Shrine Room
-------------------
Fantasy:
A shrine lets the player deliberately make the next room or next few rooms harder for better rewards.
This is the cleanest version of risk/reward and probably the best first non-combat room to implement.

Core interaction:
- Shrine offers 2-3 risk contracts.
- Each contract modifies future rooms and rewards.
- Player can accept one or leave.

Possible risk contracts:
- Next room gold x2, but enemies deal +25% damage.
- Next room XP x2, but enemies move +25% faster.
- Next room drops a rare reward, but spawns an elite miniboss.
- Next 2 rooms have +50% enemy count, but reward quality increases.
- Next room has a guaranteed affix, but chest reward improves.
- Boss drops extra Echoes, but boss gains +15% HP.
- Next room has no healing pickups, but ability upgrade chance increases.
- Next room has hazard floors, but gold drops are doubled.

Difference from Cursed Reward Room:
- Cursed Reward gives reward now, danger later.
- Risk Shrine gives danger later, reward later.

Why it fits:
It lets skilled players push their luck and choose difficulty. It also avoids free power creep.

Visual language:
- A shrine with three contract flames/runes.
- Each rune shows: reward multiplier + danger modifier.
- Accepted shrine could mark the next room with a special door glow.

Implementation notes:
- This can reuse or evolve the existing Cursed Wager concept.
- Start with next-room-only contracts before multi-room contracts.
- Reward modifiers need to be easy to inspect and clear after use.
- The next room should show an intro banner explaining the active risk contract.

Good first contract set:
- Greed: next room gold x2, enemies +25% damage.
- Hunger: next room XP x2, enemies +25% move speed.
- Pride: next room spawns a miniboss, reward chest upgrades.

Debug needs:
- Force Risk Shrine room.
- Force each contract.
- Show pending contract in HUD/debug.
- Clear pending contract.


4. Relic Forge Room
-------------------
Fantasy:
A forge, cursed anvil, reliquary, or spirit smith lets the player modify relics.
This room should be exciting but should not be free power.

Core interaction:
- Player can upgrade, corrupt, reroll, fuse, or destroy relics.
- Each action costs something.
- Player can leave if they do not like the options.

Possible actions:
- Upgrade one relic by paying gold.
- Upgrade one relic by losing max HP.
- Reroll one relic into a random relic of similar tier.
- Destroy one relic for gold/Echoes/cells.
- Corrupt one relic: stronger effect, but adds a drawback.
- Fuse two weak relics into one stronger relic.
- Overcharge a relic for 3 rooms, then disable it for 1 room.

Examples:
- Sharpen Relic: pay 100 gold to improve a relic's value.
- Corrupt Relic: double a relic effect, but enemies gain +10% damage while equipped.
- Salvage Relic: destroy relic for gold and Echoes.
- Reforge Relic: reroll relic, but next room has a curse.

Why it fits:
Relics are a major roguelite identity system. A forge room makes build decisions more interesting and gives unwanted relics a use.

Visual language:
- Forge/anvil with relic slots.
- Relic inventory panel.
- Clear before/after preview.
- Warning color for corruption.

Implementation notes:
- This depends on how mature the relic system is.
- Start simple: destroy/reroll/overcharge before full relic upgrading.
- Need UI for selecting a relic.
- Need to know whether relic effects are data-driven enough to modify safely.

Good first version:
- Pick one owned relic.
- Choose one action:
  - Salvage for gold.
  - Reroll into another relic.
  - Overcharge for 2 rooms, then disable for 1 room.

Debug needs:
- Force Relic Forge room.
- Grant test relics.
- Test relic selection UI.
- Show overcharged/disabled relic state.


5. Locked Armory Room
---------------------
Fantasy:
The room contains multiple locked cases, weapon racks, sealed chests, or enchanted cages.
The player can open one safely or open more by paying increasing costs/risks.

Core interaction:
- Show 3-5 locked cases.
- Each case has a visible category or vague hint.
- Player spends a resource to open a case.
- Opening extra cases increases danger or curse.

Possible case types:
- Ability upgrade case.
- Gold case.
- Relic case.
- Class-specific upgrade case.
- Defensive upgrade case.
- High-risk cursed case.
- Mystery case.

Possible costs:
- gold
- key/token, if a key system exists later
- max HP
- current HP
- curse stack
- harder next room
- lock out another case

Possible rules:
- First case costs gold only.
- Second case adds a curse.
- Third case spawns a future elite modifier or marks next room as dangerous.
- One case is free but low-value.
- One case is cursed but high-value.

Examples:
- Open bronze case: 50 gold, small reward.
- Open silver case: 120 gold, medium reward.
- Open black case: free relic, but next room has an elite miniboss.
- Open blood case: lose 8 max HP, gain rare ability upgrade.

Why it fits:
It is readable and visual. The player instantly understands locked rewards and limited access.

Visual language:
- Rows of locked cases/chests with colored glows.
- Clear price and reward category above each case.
- Cases break/open when selected.
- Door unlocks after player leaves or makes a selection.

Implementation notes:
- This is a good room once reward generation is stable.
- Can reuse shop/card UI pieces.
- Needs persistent state so cases do not refill if re-entered.
- Should be possible to leave without buying/opening.

Debug needs:
- Force Locked Armory room.
- Generate case set.
- Force each case category.
- Reset/open cases for testing.


6. Omen / Prophecy Room
-----------------------
Fantasy:
A quiet, eerie room that shows future danger and lets the player shape the path ahead.
This is a planning room, not a reward room.

Core interaction:
- Player sees a prophecy about upcoming rooms, elite mechanics, boss modifiers, or rewards.
- Player chooses one future modifier.
- The chosen omen affects the next room, next elite, boss, or route.

Possible omen choices:
- Next elite has Hazard Floors, but drops extra gold.
- Next boss gains armor, but drops extra Echoes.
- Next 2 rooms have more enemies, but more XP.
- Next treasure room becomes cursed but higher tier.
- Reveal the key/boss path on the dungeon map.
- Convert the next Standard room into an Elite room for better rewards.
- Reduce next room enemy count, but reduce rewards.
- Choose the next room affix from 2-3 options.

Why it fits:
It gives the player strategic agency without giving free healing or raw power.
It also makes the dungeon feel more alive and less random.

Visual language:
- Crystal ball, prophecy pool, constellation wall, omen statue.
- Show future-room icons or cards.
- Use mysterious text, but keep mechanics clear.

Implementation notes:
- This can start simple: choose one modifier for the next room.
- Later, it can interact with the dungeon graph/map.
- Needs pending omen state, similar to pending risk shrine contract.
- Needs HUD/banner display when omen activates.

Good first omen set:
- Choose next room affix: Swarm / Cursed / Hazard.
- Choose next reward bonus: gold / XP / relic chance, each with a matching danger.
- Reveal boss/key route if map information becomes useful.

Debug needs:
- Force Omen room.
- Force each omen type.
- Show pending omen in HUD/debug.
- Clear pending omen.


Rejected Or Later Non-Combat Ideas
----------------------------------
These ideas are not bad, but they are weaker fits for the current direction or should come later.

Soft Healing Room
- Risk: makes the game too easy and breaks Dead Cells-style pressure.
- Could exist very rarely, but healing should have a cost or tradeoff.

Plain Rest / Camp Room
- Risk: too safe and passive.
- Better version: Sanctuary with costly choices, not free recovery.

Friendly Shop Every Few Rooms
- Risk: too much safety/economy smoothing.
- Better version: occasional shop variants with dangerous offers.

Pure Lore Room
- Risk: may feel empty if it gives no interaction.
- Better version: lore plus omen, secret, reward clue, or dangerous choice.

Training Room
- Good for tutorials/debug, but not a strong mid-dungeon roguelite room.
- Better as a hub/tooling feature or rare preview room.

Puzzle-Only Room
- Risk: can interrupt pacing if too slow.
- Better as puzzle-lite attached to a reward/cursed choice.


Combat Room Event Ideas
-----------------------
These are for later. They should appear only in some combat rooms, not every room.
Standard combat rooms should remain part of the pacing.

Room Objective Ideas
--------------------
- Survive for 45 seconds while enemies keep spawning.
- Destroy 3 corruption crystals to unlock the doors.
- Protect a shrine, NPC, or magic lantern while enemies target it.
- Kill enemies inside a ritual circle to charge the exit.
- Chase down a fleeing treasure imp before it escapes.
- Light torches or activate seals while under pressure.
- Break cursed anchors that keep doors locked.
- Capture zones by standing inside them while fighting.
- Defeat enemies in a specific order for a bonus reward.
- Clear portals before they summon too many enemies.

Interactive Hazard Ideas
------------------------
- Spike traps that pulse on/off with clear timing.
- Fire vents that burst in lanes.
- Poison pools that grow if ignored.
- Falling rocks or ceiling collapse warning circles.
- Lightning rods that zap between each other.
- Moving walls or a slowly closing arena ring.
- Explosive barrels the player can use against enemies.
- Ice patches that reduce control.
- Mud or webbed floor that slows movement.
- Wind tunnels that push the player and enemies.
- Cursed tiles that hurt both enemies and the player.
- Lava cracks that periodically flare up.

Player Advantage Toys
---------------------
- Breakable pillars that drop rocks on enemies.
- Magic cannons or ballistae with limited shots.
- One-use room relics: freeze all enemies, stun, reveal loot, or clear projectiles.
- Pushable blocks for cover or light puzzle positioning.
- Temporary gates the player can close to split enemies.
- Reflective mirrors/crystals that redirect projectiles.
- Explosive crystals that chain-react.
- Totems that buff the player if enemies are kept away.

Enemy + Room Combo Ideas
------------------------
- Archer balconies where ranged enemies start protected.
- Slime rooms with puddles that make slimes stronger.
- Wisp rooms where lamps must be broken to stop enemy healing.
- Ogre charge lanes with breakable walls.
- Shield enemies guarding crystals or portals.
- Summoner enemies tied to portals that can be shut down.
- Phantom rooms with light zones that reveal invisible enemies.
- Bomber rooms with extra explosive barrels for chaos.
- Warchief rooms where banner objects buff enemies until destroyed.
- Cyclops rooms with pillars used as laser cover.

Layout Variety Ideas
--------------------
- Rooms with islands connected by narrow bridges.
- Rooms with a central hazard and safer outer ring.
- Rooms split into two halves where enemies flank from both sides.
- Maze-like prop layouts, but still readable and not frustrating.
- Small high-pressure arenas.
- Big kiting arenas.
- Secret side alcoves with risk/reward pickups.
- Rooms with destructible cover.
- Rooms with safe zones that rotate or move.
- Rooms with multiple entrances that affect enemy spawn positions.


System Design Recommendation
----------------------------
Eventually, add a reusable RoomSpecial system for both non-combat decision rooms and combat room events.

Possible enum:
- None
- CursedReward
- MutationAltar
- RiskShrine
- RelicForge
- LockedArmory
- OmenProphecy
- CorruptionCrystals
- HazardFloor
- PortalHunt
- RitualCircle

Possible room state:
- room special type
- initialized flag
- completed/claimed flag
- selected option index
- generated reward/cost data
- active curse/risk/omen data
- positions for interactables
- any spawned object state

Useful functions:
- RollRoomSpecial(roomIdx, roomType)
- InitRoomSpecial(roomIdx)
- UpdateRoomSpecial(dt)
- DrawRoomSpecial()
- DrawRoomSpecialUi()
- IsRoomSpecialComplete()
- ApplyRoomSpecialChoice(choiceIndex)
- Save/restore room special state

Persistence requirements:
- Room specials should not reroll when leaving and re-entering.
- Claimed rewards should stay claimed.
- Accepted curses/risks/omens should persist until their duration ends.
- Locked armory cases should stay opened/closed correctly.
- If a room was skipped, it should not secretly change behind the player.

Door requirements:
- Non-combat decision rooms should usually allow leaving without choosing, unless the room fantasy specifically locks the player in.
- Combat event rooms should keep doors locked until enemies and active objectives are complete.
- Optional challenge rooms should clearly communicate when accepting a challenge will lock the room.

Banner/HUD requirements:
- Reuse the challenge banner style for room specials.
- Show title, condition, reward, cost/drawback, and duration when relevant.
- Active curses/risks/omens should have a small HUD label.
- Debug mode should show active pending modifiers.


Recommended Implementation Order
--------------------------------
1. Risk Shrine Room
- Best first choice because it is closest to existing Cursed Wager/risk systems.
- Start with next-room-only contracts.
- Gives immediate roguelite tension without huge UI complexity.

2. Cursed Reward Room
- Simple and satisfying.
- Needs reward cards and curse application.
- Great test of persistent room rewards.

3. Omen / Prophecy Room
- Adds strategic planning.
- Start with next-room modifier choice before touching the dungeon map/pathing deeply.

4. Locked Armory Room
- Strong visual identity.
- Needs reward generation and case persistence.

5. Mutation / Build Choice Room
- Very cool, but balance-sensitive.
- Start with temporary stat mutations before complex ability rewrites.

6. Relic Forge Room
- Potentially excellent, but depends on relic system maturity.
- Start with salvage/reroll/overcharge instead of full relic upgrading.


Design Guardrails
-----------------
- Do not add free mid-dungeon healing unless it is rare or costly.
- Do not make every room complicated.
- Standard rooms are allowed and important.
- Simple rooms still need engagement: layout, pacing, enemy mix, props, hidden loot, or a small interaction.
- Every non-combat room should ask for a decision, cost, tradeoff, or commitment.
- Rewards should be visible before the player accepts danger.
- Costs and curses should be clear, not vague.
- Avoid permanent run damage unless the reward is meaningful.
- Avoid choices that are mathematically obvious every time.
- Make sure players can leave many decision rooms without choosing.
- Persist room rolls so players cannot reroll by entering/exiting.
- Use debug tools to force each room type.
- Keep first versions simple and tuneable.

Short Version
-------------
The chosen non-combat direction is not safe rest rooms.
It is dangerous decision rooms.

Prioritize:
1. Risk Shrine
2. Cursed Reward
3. Omen / Prophecy
4. Locked Armory
5. Mutation / Build Choice
6. Relic Forge

The player should feel relief when a non-combat room appears, but not safety.
The room should say: you can breathe, but now you must choose what kind of trouble you want.

ADDENDUM - CLAUDE REVIEW PASS (2026-07-08)
=====================================================================
Everything above is unchanged. This section adds improvements found by
holding the plan against the systems that already exist in the codebase.
Each item lists HOW it would work and 1-2 ways to build it.


A1. Decision Rooms On The Minimap ("?" Icon)
--------------------------------------------
Why:
Route planning only becomes gameplay if the player can SEE a decision room
before arriving. "Do I detour through the ? room while I still carry a
curse?" is a real choice. Without an icon, decision rooms are just
surprises, and surprises cannot be planned around.

How it works:
- The dungeon minimap already draws per-type icons (Engine loads
  TileSet/MapIcons/NormalRoom.png, EliteRoom, ShopRoom, TreasureRoom,
  BossRoom, RestRoom). Decision rooms get their own icon.
- Recommended: a single shared "?" / rune icon for ALL decision room
  types. The player knows a decision awaits, not which one. Mystery keeps
  the reveal fun, and one icon = one new asset.
- Later, the Omen room's "reveal" option can upgrade "?" icons into their
  true type icons for the rest of the biome - this makes the map-reveal
  omen genuinely valuable instead of trivia.

Ways to build it:
Option A (minimal): keep RoomType::Rest as the slot in DungeonGen, add a
  roomSpecial field to the room / DungeonRoomState, and draw the "?" icon
  whenever roomSpecial != None. No generator changes at all.
Option B (cleaner long-term): add RoomType::Decision to the generator so
  spawn logic (no enemies, no heal pickups) is type-driven rather than
  special-cased. Costs more call-site auditing (every switch on RoomType).
Recommendation: Option A first. Option B only if Rest rooms are fully
  retired later.

Asset note: MapIcons folder needs one new PNG (e.g. DecisionRoom.png),
same dimensions/style as the existing icons.


A2. Result Toasts - Close The Risk/Reward Loop
-----------------------------------------------
Why:
The plan specs a banner when a contract STARTS but nothing when it
RESOLVES. Risk only feels rewarding if the payoff is seen and counted.
"GREED HONORED - +214 bonus gold" is the dopamine moment; without it the
player never learns whether the contract was worth it, and the system
reads as noise.

How it works:
- While a reward-modifier is active, track the BONUS portion separately
  (e.g. gold_earned_x2 - gold_earned_x1 = bonus). On contract completion
  (contracted room cleared), show a toast: contract name + honored/failed
  + concrete bonus numbers.
- On player death during a contract: no toast, contract silently ends
  (death already costs carried Echoes; do not double-punish).
- The codebase already has the exact pattern to reuse: the
  "+N Echoes banked with Zeph" toast (_cellsBankedToastAmount/Timer in
  Engine.cpp). Copy that pattern: an amount + a timer + a fade-out draw.

Ways to build it:
Option A (simple, recommended): accumulate bonus totals inside the
  pending-modifier struct (bonusGold, bonusXp, bonusEchoes ints). The
  reward hooks (Character::AddGoldFromDrop / AddCellsFromDrop / AddExp)
  already apply multipliers - add one line that also records the delta
  when a modifier is active.
Option B (event log): a generic run-event feed ("relic gained", "contract
  honored", "curse expired") drawn as stacking toasts. More reusable, more
  work. Defer unless more systems need feeds.


A3. Every Modifier Needs An In-World Tell
------------------------------------------
Why:
"Enemies deal +25% damage" as an invisible stat reads as the game
cheating. Players accept difficulty they can SEE. This also acts as a
healthy design constraint: if an effect cannot be shown, it should not
ship in an option table.

How it works - tell per effect type:
- Enemy damage up      -> red/orange tint or glow outline on enemies.
- Enemy speed up       -> visible speed lines / red trail effect, or the
                          existing enrage flash treatment reused.
- Enemy HP up          -> slightly larger health bars (already tunable via
                          _healthBarHeight in Enemy base).
- Extra enemy count    -> visible on its own (more bodies), no extra tell.
- Hazard contracts     -> the hazards themselves are the tell.
- No-healing curse     -> heal drops spawn as visibly "sealed" (grayed
                          sprite, chain overlay) instead of not spawning -
                          the player SEES what the curse cost them.
- Boss armor omen      -> armor pips / gold trim on the boss health bar.

Ways to build it:
Option A (cheap, recommended): a per-enemy tint applied at spawn time in
  ConfigureSpawnedEnemy when a modifier is active. Enemies already support
  tint/variant recolors (SetVariantTier) - add a modifier tint that
  multiplies into the draw color.
Option B (richer): dedicated aura VFX via VFXManager per affected enemy.
  Prettier, costs perf and art time. Do A now, upgrade the worst readers
  to B later.

Rule for option tables going forward: every curse/contract entry in the
tables must name its tell in a "tell:" column. No tell, no entry.


A4. Hard Cap On Accepted Modifiers (Cap = 2)
---------------------------------------------
Why:
Ascension + Cursed Wager + shrine contract + cursed reward + room affix
can all multiply enemy stats. Uncapped, a greedy player creates an
unreadable, unfair-feeling spike, then blames the game. A cap keeps the
HUD lane readable (2 labels max) and bounds the balance space the tables
must be tuned against.

How it works:
- Count PLAYER-ACCEPTED dangers only: shrine contracts + reward curses +
  omens. Ascension (chosen at run start), Cursed Wager (biome-scale bet at
  Zeph), and random room affixes do NOT count toward the cap.
- At cap, decision rooms still appear but offers are gated with flavor:
  "You already carry enough burdens." The room shows its options grayed
  with that line, and the leave door is open.
- Cap value 2 to start; expose as a Balance constant so it is tunable.

Ways to build it:
Option A: a simple count over the active pending-modifier list, checked in
  the room's option-offer step.
Option B: a "burden points" system where big curses cost 2 slots. More
  expressive; not needed for v1.


A5. Class-Aware Reward Generation
----------------------------------
Why:
An "ability upgrade case" that reveals a Mage spell to a Warrior is a dead
pick and feels like a bug. The function to prevent this already exists:
ClassAllowsAbility(PlayerClass, AbilityType) in PlayerClass.cpp gates the
shop and level-up pools. Every room-special reward roll must use it.

How it works:
- Armory case generation: filter ability-upgrade case contents through
  ClassAllowsAbility + the player's learned list (upgrade cards for known
  abilities, learn cards for unknown ones - same rule the shop uses).
- Cursed Reward "ability upgrade" rewards: same filter.
- Mutation offers (later): tag each mutation with class affinities so at
  most one "flavored" offer appears per set, e.g. lifesteal trades bias to
  Warlock, trap/cooldown trades to Hunter, block/reflect to Paladin.
  Affinity biases the roll; it must never fully exclude - variety beats
  perfect theming.

Ways to build it:
Option A (recommended): reuse the exact reward-pool builders the shop and
  treasure chest already use (GenerateTreasureChestOptions in Engine
  already builds mixed upgrade+ability card sets). Room specials call the
  same builders with different weights.
Option B: a new standalone reward-table module. Only worth it if the shop
  builders prove too entangled to reuse.


A6. Duration Semantics - Define "Room" Precisely
--------------------------------------------------
Why:
"Next 2 rooms" is ambiguous, and the ambiguity WILL surface as a playtest
bug report. If walking through a shop burns a curse charge, curses become
trivially dodgeable; if entering a decision room burns a contract, the
player got nothing for their risk.

The rule (write it once, apply everywhere):
- Durations tick ONLY on COMBAT ROOM COMPLETION (the moment the room-clear
  event fires - the existing _roomClearPending flow).
- Shop, decision, and empty rooms never tick durations.
- "Next room" contracts apply to the NEXT COMBAT room entered, whenever
  that is. The pending contract stays armed while the player detours.
- Re-entering an already-cleared combat room does not tick anything.
- Boss rooms count as combat rooms (a "next room" contract can land on a
  boss - that is the player's risk to manage, and it is a great moment).
- Death clears all pending and active modifiers. No carryover to the next
  run, no extra penalty.

Ways to build it:
Option A (recommended): decrement durations in the same code path that
  sets _roomClearPending = true for dungeon rooms (single choke point,
  already the definition of "combat complete").
Option B: tick on door-exit of a cleared room. Slightly different feel
  (curse persists during victory-lap looting); not recommended - looting
  under pressure is part of the curse.


A7. Seed Room Specials From The Dungeon Seed
----------------------------------------------
Why:
Daily runs are seeded (_isDailyRun, _dailySeed, ComputeDailySeed) and are
supposed to be identical for every player that day. If room specials roll
GetRandomValue at room-entry time, two daily players diverge the moment
their entry order differs. Also, entry-time rolls are what allowed the old
elite-reroll exploit pattern - generation-time rolls kill the whole bug
class.

How it works:
- At dungeon generation time (DungeonGen.Generate), after room types are
  placed: roll WHICH rooms get specials, WHICH special type, and the
  special's option table contents (contract set, reward+curse pairings,
  armory case contents), storing them in the room/DungeonRoomState.
- Room entry then only READS stored state. "No reroll on re-entry" stops
  being a rule that needs enforcement - there is nothing to reroll.
- For determinism, derive from the run seed + room index (e.g. seed the
  roll with dungeonSeed * 31 + roomIdx) rather than global RNG order, so
  generation-order changes do not silently shuffle every daily.

Ways to build it:
Option A (recommended): extend the existing DungeonRoomState map -
  it already persists cleared/enemiesInitialized/eliteMechanic/survivors,
  so specials ride the same struct: specialType, optionData, claimed,
  selectedIndex.
Option B: store on DungeonRoom in DungeonGen itself. Cleaner conceptually
  but DungeonRoomState already exists and already survives room changes.

Note: the enemy-survivor system added 2026-07-08 already made room state
persistent - this extends that decision to specials, same philosophy.


A8. Extract The Cursed Wager Screen Into A Shared ChoiceCardScreen
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Why:
This is the single biggest cost reducer in the whole plan. Risk Shrine,
Cursed Reward, Mutation, and Omen are ALL "N cards, each with an upside
block and a downside block, pick one or leave." The Cursed Wager screen
(UpdateCurseShrine/DrawCurseShrine in Engine.cpp) already implements
exactly this with keyboard + gamepad + mouse + affordability gating +
deny-flash - input handling that is already debugged. Build the UI once;
after that, each new room is an OPTION TABLE, not a UI project.

How it works - the component's contract:
  struct ChoiceCard {
      title;                  // "Greed", "Cursed Relic", "Glass Focus"
      upsideHeader/upsideText;    // green block ("REWARD")
      downsideHeader/downsideText;// red block  ("RISK" / "CURSE" / "COST")
      costGold;               // 0 = free; gates + draws like wager cost
      enabled; disabledReason;// for cap-gating ("You carry enough burdens")
      tint;                   // per-card accent color
  };
  ChoiceCardScreen: SetTitle/SetSubtitle/SetCards(vector<ChoiceCard>),
  Update() -> returns -1 none / -2 leave / 0..N chosen, Draw(),
  supports 2-4 cards, Esc/B = leave (matching wager behavior).

Ways to build it:
Option A (recommended): refactor-first. Extract the wager's update/draw
  into the component, make Cursed Wager its first consumer (pure refactor,
  zero visual change, verifiable immediately), THEN build Risk Shrine as
  consumer #2.
Option B: build the component fresh for Risk Shrine and migrate the wager
  later. Risks two diverging card UIs living side by side; not
  recommended.

Also reusable by: the relic choice screen and treasure chest cards later,
if they ever need downside text.


A9. Edge-Case Semantics (Decide Now, Two Sentences Each)
----------------------------------------------------------
- Death during a contract/curse: everything ends silently. Death already
  costs carried Echoes; no double punishment, no carryover.
- Contract accepted, next room is a shop/decision room: contract stays
  armed and applies to the next COMBAT room (see A6).
- Contract lands on a boss room: it applies. Boss + contract is a peak
  moment, not a bug.
- Cursed Wager active + shrine contract on the same room: both apply -
  the wager does not count toward the accepted-modifier cap (A4), and the
  HUD lane lists both.
- Save/quit mid-contract: whatever run-persistence exists must include the
  modifier list; if runs are not save/resumable, ignore.
- Room special claimed, player re-enters room: interactable draws in its
  spent state (opened chest, dark shrine) - visible history, not vanished.

HUD note: the active-modifier HUD lane (small labels while curses or
contracts are live) is specced in the main doc's Banner/HUD section.
The wider HUD improvement pass is being designed separately - the lane
should be built to fit that pass.
